Understanding Taiwan Severance Pay

Under Taiwan's Labor Standards Act, employees who are terminated are entitled to severance compensation based on their length of service and the reason for termination. This calculator helps you determine your potential severance benefits according to current legal requirements. Severance pay is an important financial safety net designed to support workers during job transitions.

Severance Payment Rules in Taiwan

Taiwan's labor law provides different severance calculations depending on the circumstances of termination. For employer-initiated layoffs without just cause, workers typically receive two months of salary for the first year of service, four months for the second year, and continuing increments for additional years. When employment contracts expire and are not renewed, employees receive one month of salary per year of service. Employee resignations generally do not qualify for severance unless specific conditions are met.

Calculating Your Severance Amount

To calculate severance pay, start with your monthly base salary and multiply it by the appropriate factor based on years of service and termination type. Annual bonuses and incentives are often included in the calculation of average salary, which can increase your final severance amount. The calculator automatically applies the correct multipliers according to Taiwan Labor Standards Act provisions, ensuring accuracy in determining what you are legally entitled to receive.

Factors Affecting Severance Compensation

Several factors influence your final severance amount, including your monthly base salary, total years of continuous service, the official reason for employment termination, and any performance bonuses or incentive payments you received during employment. Employees with longer tenures generally receive higher severance packages. Additionally, the nature of termination—whether initiated by the employer, due to contract expiration, or by employee choice—significantly impacts the amount you are entitled to receive under Taiwan law.

Important Considerations

This calculator provides estimates based on standard Taiwan Labor Standards Act provisions and should not be considered legal advice. Actual severance amounts may vary depending on company policies, collective agreements, and specific circumstances of your employment. For precise calculations or disputes regarding severance pay, consult with a labor attorney or contact Taiwan's labor authority. FAQ

What is severance pay under Taiwan labor law?
Severance pay is compensation provided to employees when their employment is terminated. Under Taiwan's Labor Standards Act, it is calculated based on monthly salary and years of service, with different amounts depending on whether the employer or employee initiated the termination.
How is the severance calculation different for employer layoffs versus resignation?
Employer layoffs typically provide the highest severance (up to 2 months per year for the first year, plus more for additional years). Contract expiration provides 1 month per year. Employee resignations generally do not qualify for severance unless the employer violated labor laws or the employee can prove constructive dismissal.
Are annual bonuses included in severance pay calculations?
Yes, annual bonuses and performance incentives are typically included in the calculation of average salary for severance purposes. This can increase your final severance amount, so it's important to include them when using this calculator.
How many years of service do I need to qualify for severance?
Generally, you must have completed at least 3 months of continuous service to qualify for severance under Taiwan labor law. However, the amount increases with longer tenure, with different multipliers applied for each year of service.
What should I do if my employer disputes my severance calculation?
If there is a dispute, consult with a labor attorney or contact Taiwan's labor authority for clarification. Keep all employment records, salary slips, bonus documentation, and termination notices.
